Project Overview

The software to be created for Chocoholics Anonymous provides data-processing facilities for the organization. All providers can bill ChocAn by filling member and respective service information in their terminals. The members receiving services are sent a member report, while the providers are sent provider reports at the end of the week. Managers, also, get a summary report. In addition, all three are given an opportunity to request their respective reports anytime. ChocAn data operators can add, delete, or update member and provider records. The software keeps a provider directory that stores all provider data. Our Use Case Diagram provides a simple representation of these tasks, which are explained in depth in our Use Case Descriptions. On our report, we have listed each team member contribution. Glossary terms are also included to clarify the usage of terms used for the scope of this project.

Use Case Description

Use Case: Update Membership Records

Context: The ACME computer updates the ChocAn Data Center computer membership records with relevant information about financial procedures each evening at 9 P.M.

Actors: ACME

Main Success Scenario:

  1. ACME records every payment of membership fees.
  2. ACME suspends members whose fees are overdue.
  3. ACME reinstates suspended members who have paid what they owed.
  4. ACME compiles and organizes all the records they tampered with.
  5. ACME sends the data to the ChocAn Data Center computer membership records.
  6. The ChocAn Data Center computer membership records automatically update with the new information it received.

Extensions: N/A

Use Case: Receive Provider Report

Context: A weekly report of services provided is sent to each provider.

Actors: Provider

Main Success Scenario:

  1. The software first writes the information of the provider to their report.
  2. It then tracks and lists the service information the provider provided that week in chronological order.
  3. The software finishes the report by writing down the total amount of services provided and the total fees for the week.
  4. The software then sends the report to the provider.

Extensions:

1a. The information of the provider is missing.

    1a.1 The software notifies the provider that part of their information is missing.

    1a.2 The provider provides the necessary information.

    1a.3 The software resumes at step 1.

2a. Some service information is missing.

    2a.1. The software notifies the provider that part of the service information is missing.

    2a.2. The provider provides the necessary information.

    2a.3. The software resumes at step 2.

Use Case: Manage Members

Context: The operator needs to manage the Members within the ChocAn software.

Actors: Operator

Main Success Scenario:

  1. The ChocAn software at the ChocAn Data Center is run in interactive mode throughout the day to allow revision of the members
  2. The Operator adds all new members to the ChocAn software.
  3. The Operator deletes any members that have resigned from the program.
  4. The Operator updates the member records.

Extensions:

2.a There are no new members.

    2.a.1 The Operator resumes at step 3

3.a There are no resigning members.

    3.a.1 The Operator resumes at step 4.

Use Case: Initiate Health Care Services

Context: Member would like to receive health care services from the provider.

Actors: Provider

Main Success Scenario:

  1. The provider receives the member’s card and slides it through the terminal’s card reader.
  2. The terminal dials the ChocAn data center, and verifies the number.
  3. The terminal displays the word “Validated” to inform the provider that they may proceed.
  4. The provider proceeds with their health care services.

Extensions:

2.a The number has not been registered.

    2.a.1 The software informs the provider that the number is invalid.

    2.a.2 The provider registers the member into the system

    2.a.3 The member gives their first payment

    2.a.4 The software continues at step 3

2.b The member has been suspended, and would like to pay fees at the terminal.

    2.b.1 The software informs the provider that the member’s account is suspended.

    2.b.2 The member pays their fee.

    2.b.3 The software continues at step 3.

2.c The member has been suspended, and would like to pay fees at a later time.

    2.c.1 The software informs the provider that the member’s account is suspended.

    2.c.2 The software terminates.

Use Case: Bill ChocAn

Context: Provider needs to bill ChocAn after a health care service is provided.

Actors: Provider

Main Success Scenario:

  1. The provider receives the member’s card and slides it through the terminal’s card reader.
  2. The terminal dials the ChocAn data center, and verifies the number.
  3. The terminal displays the word “Validated” to inform the provider that they may proceed.
  4. The provider keys in the current date.
  5. The provider keys in the appropriate six digit service code corresponding to the service they provided.
  6. The software displays the name of the service associated with the service code.
  7. The software prompts the provider to verify the displayed service.
  8. The software prompts the provider for any comments they wish to add.
  9. The software writes a record to disk recording the current date, date the service was provided, provider and member numbers, the service code, and any comments the provider may have added.
  10. The software looks up the fee associated with the provided service and displays it to the provider.
  11. The provider totals this fee with the week’s previous fees, and bills ChocAn.

Extensions:

4.a The provider has entered an invalid date

    4.a.1 The software resumes at step 4.

5.a The provider has entered an invalid service code.

    5.a.1 The software resumes at step 5.

The software is displaying the incorrect service.

    7.a.1 The provider tells the software it is displaying the incorrect service.

    7.a.2 The software resumes at step 5.

8.a The comments total greater than 100 characters.

    8.a.1 The terminal prompts the provider to re-enter their comments.

    8.a.2 The provider re-enters comments.

    8.a.3 The software resumes at step 9

Use Case: Receive Member Report

Context: Provider needs to bill ChocAn after a health care service is provided.

Actors: Provider

Main Success Scenario:

  1. A request sent by a manager or by a timer to the ChocAn Data Center for a report.
  2. The software checks the ChocAn Data Center for every member or an individually requested member.
  3. The software checks member/members for at least one visit since the previous Friday at midnight.
  4. The software pulls the information of a member who has had a visit in the past week including their name, member number, street address, city, state, ZIP code, and of each visit, the date of service, a provider’s name, and service name.
  5. The software formats the information and contains the information in an email.
  6. The software sends the email of each member’s member report to their respective email address or each individually requested member report to the manager once all of the information is added.
  7. When the software detects it has either been through every member or it has fulfilled every requested report from the manager it stops.

Extensions: N/A

Use Case: Write EFT Data to Disk

Actors: Manager

Main Success Scenario:

  1. A request is sent to the ChocAn Data Center for information on services provided by providers from the week.
  2. The amount of money to be credited to each provider is written to a disk and is completed.

Use Case: Request Provider Directory

Context: A ChocAn health care provider intends to access the provider directory.

Actors: Provider

Main Success Scenario:

  1. The provider requests the software product for a Provider Directory.
  2. The Provider Directory, an alphabetically ordered list of service names and corresponding service codes and fees, is sent to the provider as an email attachment.

Extensions: N/A

Use Case: Request Summary Report

Context: A ChocAn manager receives a summary report for accounts payable.

Actors: Manager

Main Success Scenario:

  1. The main accounting procedure is run on Friday at midnight, or the manager specifically requests for the summary report to be run.
  2. The system reads the week’s file of services provided.
  3. A summary report for accounts payable is given to the manager, listing every provider to be paid that week, the number of consultations each had, and the provider’s total fee for that week.
  4. The total number of providers who provided services, the total number of consultations, and the overall fee total are printed.

Extensions: N/A

Glossary Terms

  1. Member: An entity that is enlisted to receive services provided by ChocAn.
  2. Member Number: A unique 9-digit number attributed to all providers.
  3. Member Report: A report sent to a member that details the services provided by ChocAn.
  4. Provider: A health care professional who provides services to ChocAn members.
  5. Provider Number: A unique 9-digit number attributed to all providers.
  6. Provider Directory: A file that contains all appropriate six-digit service codes and fees corresponding to a service.
  7. Service Code: A six-digit number that specifies the service provided.
  8. Provider’s Terminal: A digital terminal for providers to handle transactions for any services provided to a member.
  9. Provider Report: A report sent to a provider that details the services they provided to members of ChocAn.
  10. Manager: A person in charge of managing several different providers under ChocAn.
  11. Manager’s Terminal: A digital terminal for managers to handle managerial tasks.
  12. Summary Report: a report, given to the manager, which lists every provider to be paid a particular week, the number of consultations each had, and their total fee for that week. It also lists the total number of providers who provided services, the total number of consultations, and the overall fee total are printed.
  13. ChocAn Data Center: The main computer that holds all the information for ChocAn organization including member data, provider data, transaction data, etc.
  14. Interactive Mode: a mode available in the software that allows records of members and providers to be updated, including the addition of new entities and deletion of any entity that resigned
  15. EFT: an electronic transaction of funds
